1 What is FAITH Junior Academy's educational philosophy?

FAITH Junior Academy is built on the philosophy of holistic education that nurtures every child's unique potential. We focus on:

  • Developing all 7 CBC core competencies alongside academic excellence
  • Creating a nurturing environment that fosters curiosity and creativity
  • Building strong character and values alongside academic skills
  • Partnering with parents to ensure consistent support for each child
  • Preparing learners for future challenges through innovative teaching methods

2 What makes FAITH Junior Academy different from other schools?

Our unique approach combines several key elements:

  • Authentic CBC Implementation: We go beyond the basic curriculum to truly develop all competencies through project-based learning
  • Innovative Learning Spaces: Purpose-built classrooms and facilities designed for 21st century learning
  • Technology Integration: Strategic use of educational technology to enhance learning without replacing human interaction
  • Parent Partnership Program: Regular workshops and communication to keep parents actively involved
  • Individualized Attention: Small class sizes with a maximum of 20 students per class

3 What are your school hours and calendar?

Our school operates on a term system following the Kenyan academic calendar:

  • School Hours: 8:00 AM to 3:30 PM (Monday to Friday)
  • Extended Care: Available until 5:30 PM for working parents
  • Academic Year: Three terms (January-April, May-August, September-December)
  • Holidays: We observe all national holidays and have mid-term breaks

Note: Detailed academic calendars are provided to parents at the beginning of each term.

4 Do you provide transportation services?

Yes, we offer safe and reliable transportation services:

  • Modern, well-maintained buses with seat belts
  • Trained drivers and dedicated bus monitors
  • Coverage of major routes in Nairobi and surrounding areas
  • GPS tracking available for parents to monitor bus locations
  • Flexible transportation options (full-time or part-time)

Transportation fees are separate from tuition and vary based on distance. Contact our transportation department for route availability and pricing.

1 What is the admissions process and timeline?

Our admissions process follows 5 clear steps:

  • Step 1 - Initial Inquiry: Submit inquiry form or call admissions (1-2 days)
  • Step 2 - Campus Tour & Assessment: Visit campus, meet teachers, assessment for PP2+ (1 week)
  • Step 3 - Application Submission: Complete online application with required documents (2-3 days)
  • Step 4 - Family Interview: Meeting with admissions committee (30-45 minutes)
  • Step 5 - Enrollment: Complete fee payment and orientation (1-2 weeks)

We accept applications year-round but recommend applying early as spaces fill quickly.

2 What documents are required for admission?

Required documents include:

  • Completed application form
  • Copy of child's birth certificate
  • Copy of child's immunization card
  • 4 recent passport-sized photographs
  • Copies of parents' national IDs
  • Previous school reports (if applicable)
  • Transfer letter (for Grade 1-3 applicants)

Note: Additional documents may be required for specific circumstances. Our admissions team will provide a complete checklist.

3 Do you accept mid-year transfers?

Yes, we accept mid-year transfers based on availability:

  • We assess the child's current level and provide necessary bridging support
  • Transfers from both CBC and 8-4-4 systems are welcome
  • A placement assessment helps us determine the appropriate class
  • We offer orientation support to help children adjust to our system

Contact our admissions office to check availability and schedule an assessment for your child.

4 Is there an entrance exam or assessment?

We conduct child-friendly assessments rather than traditional exams:

  • PP1: No formal assessment - we observe social and emotional readiness
  • PP2 & Above: Friendly assessment focusing on cognitive, social, and emotional development
  • Purpose: To understand your child's learning style, not to exclude
  • Format: Play-based activities, observation, and simple tasks

The assessment helps us understand how best to support your child's learning journey with us.

1 What are the 7 CBC core competencies and how do you develop them?

The 7 CBC core competencies are:

  • Communication & Collaboration: Through group projects and presentations
  • Critical Thinking & Problem Solving: Via inquiry-based learning challenges
  • Imagination & Creativity: Through arts, design, and innovation projects
  • Citizenship: Community service and leadership opportunities
  • Learning to Learn: Metacognition and self-assessment techniques
  • Self-efficacy: Goal-setting and personal achievement tracking
  • Digital Literacy: Purposeful technology integration in learning

We develop these through project-based learning, real-world applications, and integrated curriculum design.

2 How do you assess students under CBC?

Our assessment approach is continuous and multi-dimensional:

  • Formative Assessment: Ongoing observation and feedback during learning
  • Performance Tasks: Real-world projects demonstrating competency application
  • Portfolio Assessment: Collection of student work showing growth over time
  • Self & Peer Assessment: Students learn to evaluate their own and peers' work
  • Parent Conferences: Regular meetings to discuss holistic development

We provide detailed progress reports that go beyond grades to show competency development.

3 What enrichment programs do you offer alongside CBC?

Our enrichment programs complement the CBC curriculum:

  • Sports & Physical Education: Swimming, gymnastics, soccer, basketball
  • Creative Arts: Music, drama, visual arts, dance
  • STEM Club: Robotics, coding, science experiments
  • Language Clubs: French, Swahili, sign language
  • Environmental Club: Gardening, sustainability projects
  • Leadership Program: Student council, peer mentoring

These programs are included in our fees and scheduled during the school day or as after-school activities.

1 What is included in the school fees?

Our comprehensive fees include:

  • All CBC textbooks, workbooks, and learning materials
  • Specialized CBC assessment tools and portfolio materials
  • Technology access and educational software licenses
  • All enrichment programs (sports, arts, clubs)
  • Basic stationery and art supplies
  • School events and educational trips
  • Medical insurance for school accidents
  • Parent workshops and CBC training sessions

Additional costs: Uniforms, transportation, optional after-school care, and specific optional trips.

2 Do you offer scholarships or financial aid?

Yes, we offer limited scholarships and financial aid:

  • Merit Scholarships: For exceptional academic or talent-based achievement
  • Need-Based Aid: For families demonstrating financial need
  • Sibling Discount: 10% discount for second child, 15% for third and subsequent children
  • Early Payment Discount: 5% discount for full-year fee payment in advance

Note: Scholarship applications are reviewed by a committee and awarded based on available funds. Contact our admissions office for application details and deadlines.

3 What payment plans are available?

We offer flexible payment options:

  • Annual Payment: Pay full year in advance (5% discount)
  • Termly Payment: Pay each term before it begins (standard option)
  • Monthly Installments: Spread payments over 10 months (administrative fee applies)
  • Bank Financing: Partnership with select banks for education loans

All payment plans must be approved by our finance department. Contact us to discuss the best option for your family.

1 What is the teacher-to-student ratio?

We maintain excellent teacher-to-student ratios:

  • Pre-Primary Classes: 1:10 ratio (1 teacher + 1 assistant for every 20 students)
  • Grade 1-3 Classes: 1:15 ratio maximum
  • Specialist Teachers: Additional teachers for languages, arts, PE, and technology
  • Support Staff: Learning support assistants available for additional help

Small class sizes ensure personalized attention and optimal learning conditions for every child.

2 How do you handle discipline and behavior management?

We use positive discipline approaches aligned with CBC values:

  • Restorative Practices: Focusing on repairing harm and restoring relationships
  • Positive Behavior Support: Recognizing and reinforcing good behavior
  • Social-Emotional Learning: Teaching self-regulation and conflict resolution skills
  • Clear Expectations: Age-appropriate rules developed with student input
  • Parent Partnership: Working together to support consistent behavior expectations

Our approach focuses on teaching appropriate behavior rather than punishment.

3 How do you communicate with parents about student progress?

We maintain open, regular communication through:

  • Parent Portal: Online access to assignments, grades, and announcements
  • Weekly Updates: Email newsletters with class activities and upcoming events
  • Termly Reports: Detailed progress reports on academic and competency development
  • Parent-Teacher Conferences: Scheduled meetings each term
  • Open Door Policy: Parents can schedule meetings with teachers as needed
  • School Events: Performances, exhibitions, and open days

We believe strong parent-school partnership is essential for student success.
