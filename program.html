Understanding the CBC Approach

Moving beyond memorization to meaningful competency development

What is Competency-Based Curriculum?

The Competency-Based Curriculum (CBC) is Kenya's innovative educational framework designed to nurture every child's potential through a flexible, learner-centered approach. Unlike traditional systems that prioritize exam performance, CBC focuses on developing practical skills, knowledge, and values that learners can apply in real-life situations.

At FAITH Junior Academy, we've embraced CBC not just as a curriculum but as a philosophy. We go beyond the basic requirements to create learning experiences that are engaging, relevant, and transformative.

The 7 Core Competencies

Building the foundation for lifelong learning and success

Communication & Collaboration

Developing effective communication skills in English, Kiswahili, and Kenyan Sign Language, along with teamwork abilities for productive collaboration.

Critical Thinking & Problem Solving

Nurturing analytical skills, creativity, and innovation to tackle complex challenges and make informed decisions in various contexts.

Imagination & Creativity

Encouraging original thinking, artistic expression, and innovative approaches to learning and problem-solving across all subjects.

Citizenship

Developing responsible, ethical citizens who understand their rights, responsibilities, and role in building a just, cohesive society.

Digital Literacy

Building proficiency in using digital tools responsibly, creatively, and effectively for learning, communication, and productivity.

Learning to Learn

Developing self-awareness, metacognitive skills, and resilience to become independent, self-directed lifelong learners.

Self-Efficacy

Building confidence, perseverance, and a growth mindset to set and achieve personal and academic goals.

Integrity & Values

Cultivating strong moral principles, empathy, respect, and responsibility in personal conduct and community interactions.

Pre-Primary 1 (Age 4)

Foundational stage focusing on play-based learning, social skills, and basic literacy/numeracy readiness.

Language Activities

Introduction to English and Kiswahili through songs, stories, and conversations.

  • Listening and speaking skills
  • Basic vocabulary development
  • Storytelling and dramatic play
  • Pre-writing activities

Mathematical Activities

Developing early numeracy concepts through play and exploration.

  • Sorting and classifying objects
  • Basic counting 1-20
  • Simple patterns and sequences
  • Shape and space awareness

Creative Activities

Exploring various art forms and developing fine motor skills.

  • Drawing, painting, and collage
  • Music and movement
  • Role play and drama
  • Construction with blocks

Social & Life Skills

Developing independence, cooperation, and self-awareness.

  • Personal hygiene routines
  • Sharing and taking turns
  • Expressing feelings appropriately
  • Basic safety awareness

Pre-Primary 2 (Age 5)

Building on foundational skills with more structured learning activities and school readiness.

Language & Literacy

Developing early reading and writing readiness in English and Kiswahili.

  • Phonemic awareness activities
  • Letter recognition and sounds
  • Simple sentence formation
  • Listening comprehension

Mathematics & Environment

Exploring numbers, measurements, and the natural world.

  • Number operations 1-50
  • Basic addition and subtraction
  • Measurement concepts
  • Exploring plants and animals

Creative & Physical

Developing artistic expression and physical coordination.

  • Rhythm and musical patterns
  • Creative storytelling
  • Gross motor skill development
  • Team games and activities

Social & Moral Development

Cultivating positive relationships and ethical awareness.

  • Conflict resolution skills
  • Understanding community roles
  • Basic digital literacy introduction
  • Cultural appreciation

The Parental Role in CBC

Partnership between home and school for optimal learner development

Learning Environment at Home

Creating a supportive space for homework, projects, and skill practice. We provide parents with guidance on setting up effective learning corners and routines.

Supporting Projects & Assignments

Guiding learners through CBC projects without doing the work for them. We conduct regular parent workshops on effective support strategies.

Regular Communication

Staying informed through our parent portal, regular meetings, and progress reports. We value parental feedback and insights about learner development.

Skill Reinforcement

Identifying and nurturing your child's talents through extracurricular activities and real-life skill application at home and in the community.

Progress Monitoring

Understanding CBC assessment methods and tracking your child's development across competencies, not just academic scores.

Values & Character Building

Partnering with us to reinforce core values, social skills, and emotional intelligence through consistent messaging and modeling.

CBC Assessment Framework

Continuous, competency-based evaluation that tracks holistic development

Formative Assessment

Ongoing evaluation through observations, discussions, and learning activities. Teachers provide immediate feedback to guide learning progression and identify support needs.

PP1 - Grade 6

Projects & Portfolios

Learners compile evidence of their learning through projects, artwork, writing samples, and multimedia presentations that demonstrate competency development over time.

PP2 - Grade 6

Peer & Self-Assessment

Learners develop metacognitive skills by evaluating their own work and providing constructive feedback to peers, fostering responsibility and critical thinking.

Grade 1 - Grade 6

Summative Assessment

End-of-term evaluations that measure competency achievement across learning areas, providing comprehensive profiles of learner development.

PP2 - Grade 6

School-Based Assessment

Classroom assessments approved by KICD that contribute to the final learner profile, focusing on practical application of knowledge and skills.
